Marin Headlands

A winding five-mile drive through some of the most stunning stretches of road in California.

Sausalito, California 94965

(415) 331-1540

www.nps.gov

Vast and open
    The crowd

    Must-see

    Golden Gate views

    Getting around

    Plenty to see by car, much more by foot

    Best for

    Anyone who wants that perfect Golden Gate shot

Our Review

By
Carey JonesContributing Writer

Let’s start big picture. What’s the vibe here?San Francisco is a beautiful city to visit, but perhaps it may be even more beautiful from a distance. The Marin Headlands, immediately north the Golden Gate Bridge, are a vast stretch of National Park with unobstructed (when there's no fog) views of the Pacific, the city, and the Golden Gate. A winding five-mile drive takes you through one of the most stunning stretches of road in California. If you'd rather get out of the car for more than a photo op, there are numerous hiking trails, beaches, and campgrounds.

Any standout features or must-sees?Whether you're here for 10 minutes or 10 hours, your first stop should be the Golden Gate Viewpoint, which offers the region's best view of the city. Steady-footed visitors can also take the steep and rugged hike out to the Point Bonita Lighthouse.

Was it easy to get around?Major sites like the Golden Gate View Point and the Marin Headlands Visitor Center are easy to access by car; they're well-marked and have good facilities. Once you're out onto the trails, the landscape is less navigable and facilities are generally limited to trailheads.

All said and done, what—and who—is this best for?If you have access to a car, it's worth the 20-minute drive over the bridge and back; you'll be able to snap a quick shot of the Golden Gate while soaking in the rolling-hill scenery of Northern California. For outdoorsy types, the park offers endless hiking and camping opportunities, all close to the city.
